Receiving email from this domain?

Only the domain paulhefford.com (not including subdomains) is used to send email, however all the domains I own publish SPF, DKIM and DMARC records in DNS so the source of email can be verified. Any email which appears to be sent from the domain paulhefford.com is expected to pass both SPF and DKIM tests as per the DMARC policy. If either of these tests does not pass, the email should be rejected as it did not originate from an authorised source.

Many email systems as well as large email providers such as Google, Yahoo! and Microsoft incorporate the checks mentioned above into their spam detection rules, so no explicit recipient action is normally required. Unfortunately, because the sender address of an email can easily be forged, I cannot prevent unauthorised emails from being sent. By publishing the aforementioned email validation information, recipients are able to quickly and unobtrusively validate email which is apparently sent from my domains.
